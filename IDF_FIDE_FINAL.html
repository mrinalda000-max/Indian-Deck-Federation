Single Knockout
FCL · Grenke Classic · Chennai Masters
Classical / Rapid / Blitz
FORMAT
Single-elimination bracket. One loss = eliminated. No second chances.
MATCH STRUCTURE
Best of 2 games per match. If tied after 2 games → tiebreak (rapid/blitz armageddon).
RATING
FCL → Rapid rated (Grand Prix circuit)
Grenke Classic → Classical rated (Grand Coco Tour)
Chennai Masters → Blitz rated (Grand Slam)
WINNER
Last player standing wins the event. Earns an individual win and rating points.
Single Round Robin
St. Louis Rapid · St. Louis Blitz · Sinquefield Cup
Rapid / Blitz / Classical
FORMAT
Every player plays every other player exactly once. Most points wins.
SCORING
Win = 1 point · Draw = 0.5 · Loss = 0. Tiebreak by head-to-head, then Sonneborn-Berger.
RATING
St. Louis Rapid → Rapid rated
St. Louis Blitz → Blitz rated
Sinquefield Cup → Classical rated
CIRCUITS
St. Louis Rapid → Grand Prix sub-event
St. Louis Blitz → Grand Slam sub-event
Sinquefield → Grand Coco Tour sub-event
Swiss System
Tata Steel Masters · Norway Classic · Grunfeld Cup
Classical / Blitz
FORMAT
Players are paired each round based on current score. No player faces the same opponent twice.
ROUNDS
Tata Steel Masters → 7 rounds
Norway Classic → 6 rounds
Grunfeld Cup → 7 rounds
RATING
Tata Steel → Classical rated (Grand Coco Tour)
Norway Classic → Classical rated (Grand Coco Tour)
Grunfeld Cup → Blitz rated (Grand Slam)
SCORING
Win = 1 · Draw = 0.5 · Loss = 0. Tiebreak by Buchholz score.
Grand Swiss
18 players · Cross-group event · Candidates Spots A
Semi-Classical · Classical Rated
QUALIFICATION
Top 18 players by Classical Elo overall (across all groups) qualify.
FORMAT
Round 1: Seeding/qualifier round — players ranked by Classical Elo.
Rounds 2–7: Swiss system — 6 rounds.
TIME CONTROL
Semi-Classical — faster than full classical but rated as Classical Elo. Designed for quicker decisive results.
CANDIDATES SPOTS
Winner → Spot A1
Runner-up → Spot A2
No rolldown — both spots always filled from Grand Swiss.
World Cup
16 players · Cross-group event · Candidates Spots B
Double Knockout · Classical Rated
QUALIFICATION
Top 16 players by Classical Elo overall qualify.
FORMAT
Double elimination. Each player gets two chances — one loss drops to the losers bracket. Two losses = eliminated.
RATING
Fully Classical rated. All games count towards Classical Elo.
CANDIDATES SPOTS · ROLLDOWN
Winner → Spot B1 · Runner-up → Spot B2
If already qualified: take #3.
If #3 also qualified: no spot awarded from World Cup.
Rapid Championship
8 players · Cross-group · Candidates Spot C
Rapid Rated
QUALIFICATION
Top 2 Rapid Elo from each group = 8 players total.
FORMAT
Format changes each cycle — may be Round Robin, Swiss, Knockout or hybrid. Announced per cycle.
CANDIDATES SPOT
Winner → Spot C1. If already qualified via A or B, spot is skipped entirely — no rolldown.
Blitz Championship
8 players · Cross-group · Candidates Spot C
Blitz Rated
QUALIFICATION
Top 2 Blitz Elo from each group = 8 players total.
FORMAT
Not a fixed format — changes every cycle. Could be any competitive format. Announced per cycle.
CANDIDATES SPOT
Winner → Spot C2. If already qualified via A or B, spot is skipped entirely — no rolldown.
Candidates Tournament
5–8 players · Determines Championship challenger
Semi-Classical · Classical Rated
FORMAT
Generally Double Round Robin — each player faces every other player twice (once with each colour). May change per cycle.
TIME CONTROL
Semi-Classical — faster than full classical, rated as Classical Elo. High-stakes, decisive format.
WINNER
Highest score wins and earns the right to challenge the reigning Classic Champion.
REPUTATION
Considered the hardest event in COCO. Winning the Candidates is arguably more difficult than the Championship itself.
Classic Championship Match
The title match · Candidates winner vs Reigning Champion
UNRATED
STRUCTURE
12 Classical games. Played over multiple sessions. No time pressure on format.
WINNING CONDITION
Challenger needs 6.5 points to win the title.
Champion needs only 6 points to retain.
A 6–6 tie = Champion retains the title.
RATING
Unrated. The Championship match does not affect any Elo — Classical, Rapid, or Blitz.
TITLE IMPLICATIONS
Winner earns the GM title if not already held. 3 consecutive wins → SGM. 10 consecutive wins → D'.
Records
Official FIDE records in COCO — Call of Cards Official. All records current as of Cycle 26.
RECORD 01
Most Classic Championship Titles
20
D' Mrinal
Won the Classic Championship 20 times across all three sagas — Cycles 2 through 24. The next highest active player has 1 title. A record that defines the entire history of COCO.
RECORD 02
Most Consecutive Classic Championship Wins
12
D' Mrinal
Twelve consecutive Classic Championship victories from Cycle 13 to Cycle 24, spanning Sagas 2 and 3. This streak directly earned the D' title at the 10th consecutive win. To break this record, a player must win 13 championships in a row — an achievement with no precedent in COCO history.
RECORD 03
Most Individual Event Wins (All-Time)
44
D' Mrinal
44 individual event wins across all sagas — championships, candidates, blitz, rapid, invitationals and more. The next closest active player has 18. At the rate of even 3 wins per cycle, it would take a Phase 4 player over 8 full cycles to approach this tally.
RECORD 04
Most Consecutive Candidates Tournament Wins
4
CM Dixita
Four consecutive Candidates Tournament victories — Cycles 16, 17, 18 and 19. The Candidates is widely regarded as the most competitive event in COCO, with the full field of qualified players competing each cycle. No other player has won it more than twice in a row.
RECORD 05
Most Championship Match Appearances
23
D' Mrinal
Appeared in the Classic Championship title match 23 times — as challenger and as defending champion. Of those, 20 resulted in victories. This record reflects not just dominance but sustained elite-level performance across the entirety of COCO's competitive history.
RECORD 06
Only Perfect Score in a Championship Match
6–0
D' Mrinal vs CM Henthoi · Cycle 23
The only instance in COCO history of a player winning a 12-game Championship match without conceding a single point. Mrinal defeated CM Henthoi 6–0 in Cycle 23. In a format where the champion needs only 6 points to retain, a clean sweep of this nature has never been repeated and stands as the most dominant single-match performance on record.

Candidates Qualification System
Spots awarded in order A → B → C → D
SPOT A — GRAND SWISS
Winner + Runner-up of Grand Swiss. No rolldown — both spots always filled.
SPOT B — WORLD CUP
Winner + Runner-up. If winner already qualified → take #3. If #3 also qualified → no World Cup spot awarded.
SPOT C — RAPID + BLITZ CHAMPIONS
One spot each. If champion already qualified via A or B → spot is skipped entirely (no rolldown).
SPOT D — CLASSICAL ELO LEADERBOARD
#1 + #2 Classical Elo (post Rapid/Blitz Championships). Roll to #3 if qualified. If still under 5 players total: previous Candidates winner gets a spot, then #4 rated. Stop after this regardless.
